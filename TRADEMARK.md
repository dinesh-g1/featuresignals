## 2. General Principles

1. **FeatureSignals Marks are valuable assets.** Use them only as authorized by this policy or by explicit written permission from FeatureSignals Inc.
2. **Do not dilute or tarnish the brand.** Use FeatureSignals Marks in a way that does not mislead, confuse, or imply false endorsement.
3. **Distinguish FeatureSignals Marks.** Always capitalize and/or stylize FeatureSignals Marks per our brand guidelines. Do not incorporate them into your own product, service, company, or domain name.
4. **Attribute ownership.** Include a notice attributing the mark to FeatureSignals Inc. on any materials that reference FeatureSignals Marks.

## 3. Allowed Uses (Without Prior Written Permission)

### 3.1 Nominative (Fair) Use
You may use FeatureSignals Marks to accurately refer to FeatureSignals products or services, provided that:

- The use is truthful and not misleading.
- The use does not imply sponsorship, endorsement, or affiliation with FeatureSignals Inc.
- The FeatureSignals Mark is used less prominently than your own brand.
- The FeatureSignals Mark is accompanied by a notice such as:  
  *"FeatureSignals is a registered trademark of FeatureSignals Inc."*

### 3.2 Open Source Distribution
You may distribute unmodified copies of **FeatureSignals Community Edition** under the Apache 2.0 license, provided that:

- You do not remove, modify, or obscure any trademark or copyright notices in the software.
- You clearly mark any modifications you make, and do not imply that FeatureSignals Inc. endorses those modifications.
- You do not use FeatureSignals Marks in the name of your distribution (e.g., `"Acme's FeatureSignals"` is not permitted).

### 3.3 Compatibility and Interoperability
You may use FeatureSignals Marks to truthfully describe compatibility or interoperability, for example:

- ✅ "Works with FeatureSignals"
- ✅ "Compatible with FeatureSignals Community Edition"
- ❌ "FeatureSignals-compatible server" (implies certification or endorsement)

### 3.4 Community Contributions
- You may use "FeatureSignals" in the name of community meetups, user groups, or online forums, provided the name follows the format:  
  `"[Location] FeatureSignals Meetup"` or `"FeatureSignals [Topic] Discussion"`
- You must include a disclaimer that the group is unofficial and not affiliated with FeatureSignals Inc.

### 3.5 Blog Posts, Articles, and Commentary
You may use FeatureSignals Marks in titles and content of blog posts, articles, books, or presentations that discuss FeatureSignals, provided:

- The content is factual and not misleading.
- The FeatureSignals Mark is not used as part of your brand or product name.
- If the content is critical or negative, you still comply with this policy — fair criticism is protected.

## 4. Prohibited Uses (Without Prior Written Permission)

### 4.1 Domain Names and Subdomains
You **must not** register or use any domain name, subdomain, or URL that contains a FeatureSignals Mark or a confusingly similar variant, including but not limited to:

- `featuresignals-{keyword}.com`
- `getfeaturesignals.com`
- `fs-{product}.io`
- `featuresignals.cloud`
- `tryfeaturesignals.com`

### 4.2 Product and Service Names
You **must not** use a FeatureSignals Mark as part of your product, service, package, library, or project name. This includes:

- `FeatureSignals Pro` (confusingly similar to Enterprise Edition)
- `FeatureSignals Manager`
- `FeatureSignals Dashboard Plus`
- `FS-Extended`

### 4.3 Company Names and Legal Entities
You **must not** use a FeatureSignals Mark or a confusingly similar term in your company name, trade name, DBA, or legal entity name.

### 4.4 Implying Endorsement or Certification
You **must not** imply that FeatureSignals Inc. sponsors, endorses, certifies, or approves of your product, service, or organization without explicit written permission.

Prohibited examples:
- ❌ "Certified FeatureSignals Partner" (without agreement)
- ❌ "Official FeatureSignals Plugin"
- ❌ "FeatureSignals Recommended"

### 4.5 Modification or Derogatory Use
You **must not**:
- Modify, distort, or alter FeatureSignals Marks (including colors, proportions, or adding text/graphics).
- Use FeatureSignals Marks in a defamatory, derogatory, or obscene context.
- Combine FeatureSignals Marks with your own marks in a way that creates a composite mark.

### 4.6 Commercial Hosting or SaaS
You **must not** offer FeatureSignals Community Edition as a hosted or managed service under a name that includes or implies "FeatureSignals" (e.g., `"FeatureSignals as a Service"`, `"Managed FeatureSignals"`, `"FeatureSignals Cloud"`).

If you want to offer such a service, you **must**:
1. Use your own brand as the primary name.
2. Include a clear statement that the service is powered by FeatureSignals Community Edition.
3. Open-source any modifications under Apache 2.0.
4. Enter into a license agreement with FeatureSignals Inc. for commercial hosting rights.

### 4.7 False or Misleading Claims
You **must not** make false or misleading claims about FeatureSignals, including:
- Falsely claiming a product is "FeatureSignals-compatible" or "certified by FeatureSignals"
- Implying a version is the "Enterprise Edition" when it is not
- Claiming affiliation with FeatureSignals Inc. without authorization

### 4.8 Usage in Software Package Names
You **must not** use `@featuresignals`, `featuresignals`, `fs-`, or similar prefixes in package registries (npm, PyPI, Maven, RubyGems, NuGet, Docker Hub, etc.) for packages that are not officially published by FeatureSignals Inc.

## 5. Logo and Visual Brand Assets

### 5.1 Official Assets
All FeatureSignals brand assets (logos, icons, color palette, typography) are available at:  
`https://brand.featuresignals.com/`

### 5.2 Permitted Logo Use
You may use the FeatureSignals logo:
- In a blog post, article, or presentation that discusses FeatureSignals.
- On a website to link to the FeatureSignals website or GitHub repository.
- In a compatibility or interoperability notice (e.g., "Works with FeatureSignals" badge).
- At community events, meetups, and hackathons where FeatureSignals is featured.

### 5.3 Logo Usage Rules
- Always use the official logo files. Do not recreate, trace, or modify the logo.
- Maintain clear space around the logo equal to at least the height of the logomark's "F".
- Do not display the logo smaller than 24px in digital media.
- Do not use the logo as a background pattern or texture.
- On dark backgrounds, use the white/light version of the logo. On light backgrounds, use the dark version.

### 5.4 Logo Prohibitions
- Do not animate, rotate, or apply visual effects to the logo.
- Do not place the logo on a busy or clashing background.
- Do not add text, emojis, or other elements on or overlapping the logo.
- Do not use the logo as a favicon for non-FeatureSignals websites.
- Do not use the logo in a way that suggests endorsement of a product or service.

## 7. Enforcement

FeatureSignals Inc. actively monitors and enforces its trademark rights. We take the following approach:

### 7.1 Monitoring
- Automated monitoring of domain registrations, package registries, and social media platforms.
- Community reporting via `trademarks@featuresignals.com`.
- Regular audits of GitHub, npm, PyPI, Docker Hub, and other registries.

### 7.2 Infringement Response
- **First offense (unintentional):** We will send a polite notice requesting compliance. We aim to resolve issues amicably.
- **Repeated or willful infringement:** We will send a formal cease-and-desist letter and pursue all available legal remedies, including but not limited to:
  - Domain name dispute resolution (UDRP)
  - DMCA takedown notices
  - Package registry takedown requests
  - Trademark litigation

### 7.3 What to Report
If you see unauthorized use of FeatureSignals Marks, please report it to `trademarks@featuresignals.com` with:
- The URL or location of the infringement
- Screenshots or detailed description
- Your contact information
